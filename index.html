D3.js "web site health check" - Treemap, shaded, from hierarchical JSON

Source: Nathan Yau's "Jobs Charted by State and Salary."

Whole-ecology treemap visualization to surface quality problems in a large web site. Foundational data - content inventory plus traffic - can be paired with one or more error reports such as broken links. Helps product managers comprehend what their highest-severity problems are, and helps them decide what communication packages (thematic groups of pages) should be fixed first. Here, points are used instead of item counts, so management is able to shift work priorities by raising the severity of one or more error types.

This tool has been scaled effectively to a site of 30,000 static HTML pages grouped into 420 thematic groups with 16 owners (what is shown as "topics" in this version of the map are organization owners in the production map) with dozens of product managers. The JSON is hierarchical, allowing one topic to become the whole map (not implemented here). Each content group is a member of a topic category. The database includes multiple error / fix reports, including:

  • Broken links
  • Outdated video formats
  • Content not reviewed by a subject specialist in the past 12 months
  • Pages linking to Word, Excel, and other "non-web" documents that should be evaluated for conversion to HTML
  • Low percentage of task completion in customer survey
  • Low satisfaction in customer survey
